⭐ Featured · AI/ML · EdTech

Smart Curriculum Recommender (SCR)

A 3-layer hybrid AI personalisation engine built for Chegg Skills — solving "catalog overwhelm" by guiding students from unclear intent to the right course in seconds, with a structured path toward their career goal.

Problem
Students visiting Chegg Skills faced catalog overload — hundreds of courses with no personalised entry point. Most knew what they wanted to achieve (become a Data Scientist, switch careers) but not which courses to buy or in what order. Discovery friction was the primary driver of abandonment before first purchase — not lack of content, but lack of navigation.
The 3-Layer AI Engine
Layer 1 — Content-Based Filter: Converts student profiles and courses into weighted feature vectors; ranks via cosine similarity. Solves cold-start — works with zero prior history. "Growth zone" bonus targets content slightly above the student's current level.

Layer 2 — Collaborative SVD: 10K-student × 173-course implicit feedback matrix, factorised with Truncated SVD into 128 latent factors (83% variance explained). Surfaces what students who behaved similarly found valuable.

Layer 3 — Sequential Markov: Learns bigram transition probabilities from completion sequences — 1,077 bigram states predicting the optimal next step. Activates after 2+ completions.

Dynamic blending: Stage-based weights auto-shift from cold-start (100% content-based) to experienced (50% sequential + 30% collaborative + 20% content). No manual configuration.
Tools
PythonScikit-learn (SVD)Pandas / NumPy Cosine SimilarityMarkov Chains FastAPIDocker Google ColabPower BI
Key Learnings
Personalisation only holds value when backed by a feedback loop — the refresh mechanism was the critical design decision. Subscribers completed 30% more courses than free-tier users, validating "navigation as a product" distinct from the courses themselves.

Building for all lifecycle stages simultaneously — not the average user — was the core architectural insight. Cold-start users need content-based filtering; experienced users need sequential patterns.

📑 Research · EDA · Published

EDA on Alcohol Consumption & Academic Performance

Existing studies ignored demographic variation, urban/rural differences, and social behaviour as combined predictors of alcohol consumption in students.

Approach

Python-based EDA on 649 student records from Portuguese secondary schools. Statistical visualisation (Seaborn, Matplotlib), correlation heatmaps, hypothesis testing across demographic, academic, and social dimensions. Published ICIMMI 2022.

Tools
PythonPandasSeabornMatplotlibGoogle Colab
Key Findings
Urban → more consumptionFamily quality → ↓ drinking
Key Learning

Non-linear dynamics are missed by simple models. The peak appeared at famrel=2, not famrel=1 — strong relationships reduce consumption, but the pattern isn't monotonic.
